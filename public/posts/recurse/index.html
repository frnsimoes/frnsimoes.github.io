My Experience at the Recurse Center

My Recurse Center Experience

The Recurse Center is a self-directed, community-driven educational retreat for programmers in New York City (and now also remote!). I attended RC for a batch in [insert your batch date here], and it was one of the most enriching experiences of my programming career.

What is the Recurse Center?

RC is often described as a “writer’s retreat for programmers.” There are no teachers, no curriculum, and no grades. Instead, RC provides a supportive environment where you can work on whatever programming projects interest you most.

The community operates on a few simple social rules:

  • No feigning surprise
  • No well-actually’s
  • No back-seat driving
  • No subtle -isms

These rules help create a space where people can learn without fear of judgment.

The Community

The best part of RC is undoubtedly the community. I met programmers from diverse backgrounds with varying interests and expertise. The collaborative atmosphere meant that there was always someone willing to help with a challenging problem or provide feedback on a project.

Daily check-ins, interest groups, and social activities helped foster connections beyond just programming.

Should You Apply?

If you’re someone who:

  • Loves programming and wants to become a better programmer
  • Is self-directed and motivated
  • Values learning in community
  • Wants to dedicate time to exploring your programming interests

Then RC might be a good fit for you. The application process involves a written application and technical interviews, but they’re looking for curiosity and passion more than specific technical skills.

Conclusion

My time at RC was transformative. I became a better programmer, made lasting friendships, and gained a community that continues to support my growth. If you’re considering applying, I enthusiastically recommend it.
