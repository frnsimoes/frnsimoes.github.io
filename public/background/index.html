I think the best way to get to know me is to get a brief summary of what has caught my attention in the past years:

2009-2011 In 2009, I became interested in philosophy. What I wanted to understand the most was the nature of reality. It was a big enterprise for the little man that I was (only 20 years old). Still, I think I achieved some nice accomplishments back then and learned some Greek to read Plato and Aristotle. I didn’t delve too deep into my Greek studies, but I read these two philosophers for about 3 years, almost exclusively.

2011 A friend introduced me to a professor from UFPR (Universidade Federal do Paraná), a well-known university in Curitiba, Paraná. He had been teaching analytic philosophy for 20 years or more, and we instantly became friends. He taught me a lot about analytic philosophy and logic. I read a bunch of Saul Kripke and understood the importance of Philosophy of Language in the modern sense. I got interested in the philosophy of language, and how things can have different meanings.

2012-2015 Until 2015, I almost exclusively read literature. I started with Homer and got hooked on Russian literature. I read almost everything that was translated: Dostoevsky, Tolstoy, Chekhov, Oblomov, and so many others.

2016-2017 I went through a Shakespearean crusade. I decided I was going to read everything from the source. In the original version. And I did it. I bought a lot of Shakespeare-specific dictionaries, bought the classic editions (from Arden and Folger to the New Variorum). I wrote a little about it in this post. I also published a short essay on Macbeth in a local journal.

2018 Shakespeare sparked a deep interest in the human heart in me. While working as a lawyer, I became really interested in how the brain works, the nature of our problems as human beings, and how to live in the best way possible.
