I think the best way to get to know me is to get a brief summary of what has caught my attention in the past years:

**2009-2011** In 2009, I became interested in philosophy. What I wanted to understand the most was the nature of reality. It was a big enterprise for the little man that I was (only 20 years old). Still, I think I achieved some nice accomplishments back then and learned some Greek to read Plato and Aristotle. I didn't delve too deep into my Greek studies, but I read these two philosophers for about 3 years, almost exclusively.

**2011** A friend introduced me to a professor from UFPR (Universidade Federal do Paraná), a well-known university in Curitiba, Paraná. He had been teaching analytic philosophy for 20 years or more, and we instantly became friends. He taught me a lot about analytic philosophy and logic. I read a bunch of Saul Kripke and understood the importance of Philosophy of Language in the modern sense. I got interested in the philosophy of language, and how things can have different meanings. 

**2012-2015** Until 2015, I almost exclusively read literature. I started with Homer and got hooked on Russian literature. I read almost everything that was translated: Dostoevsky, Tolstoy, Chekhov, Oblomov, and so many others. 2015 was the year I bought my first Unix system and started scripting something to automate my daily life.

**2016-2017** I went through a Shakespearean crusade. I decided I was going to read everything from the source. In the original version. And I did it. I bought a lot of Shakespeare-specific dictionaries, bought the classic editions (from Arden and Folger to the New Variorum). I wrote a little about it [in this post](https://fsimoesgarcia.medium.com/a-pregui%C3%A7osos-e-diletantes-shakespeare-51d18ca52377). I also published a [short essay](https://www.jornalopcao.com.br/opcao-cultural/as-criancas-e-que-temem-ver-o-diabo-pintado-reflexoes-shakespeareanas-parte-1-90411/) on Macbeth at a local journal

**2018** Shakespeare sparked a deep interest in the human heart in me. While working as a lawyer, I became really interested in how the brain works, the nature of our problems as human beings, and how to live in the best way possible. I read a lot of the classics, especially Adler. I also got in contact with Freud and Jung, and read some of the modern essays on psychology.
